## Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is one of the most ancient dance styles of India, which originated in the temples of Tamil Nadu. This highly stylized classical Indian dance is renowned for its grace, purity, tenderness, and sculpturesque poses. The name Bharatanatyam is derived from the words Bhava (expression), Raga (music), Tala (rhythm), and Natyam (dance).

> "Yatho Hasta thatho Drishti, Yatho Drishti thatho Manah, Yatho Manah thatho Bhaava, Yatho Bhaava thatho Rasa."
>
> — Nandikesvara, Abhinaya Darpana
>
> *"Where the hands go, the eyes follow; where the eyes go, the mind follows; where the mind goes, feeling is born; where feeling is born, mood (rasa) is created."*

The dance form is accompanied by Carnatic music and involves complex footwork, expressive hand gestures (mudras), and dramatic facial expressions (abhinaya). Lord Shiva is considered the supreme dancer in the form of Nataraja.

## Natesha School

> "Angikam bhuvanam yasya, Vachikam sarva vangmayam, Aharyam chandra taradi, Tam namah satvikam sivam."
>
> *"He whose limbs are the world, whose song and poetry are the essence of all language, whose costume is the moon and stars — I bow to Him, the benevolent Shiva."*

Natesha School of Bharatanatyam was founded in 2001 by Guru Mrs. Suman Nayak with the objective of teaching the art of Bharatanatyam to students in San Diego, California. The school follows the Pandanallur style of Bharatanatyam and is committed to teaching the art in its purest form.

The school conducts classes in Poway and Scripps Ranch. Students of all ages and backgrounds are welcome. The curriculum progresses from basic adavus through margam, culminating in the Arangetram (solo debut performance) for dedicated students.

## Guru — Suman Nayak

Guru Suman Nayak has been training in Bharatanatyam for over 30 years. She trained for 16 years in the Pandanallur tradition under the guidance of Sri U.K. Praveen of Nritya Vidya Nilaya, Bangalore. She has also trained under Smt. Ramya Harishankar of Los Angeles.

Guru Suman Nayak is well known in Southern California for her fluid grace and rhythmic clarity, and her ability to blend creative choreography within the traditional Pandanallur framework. She has performed in numerous IFAASD events and Indian cultural programs throughout San Diego since 2001.
