# How to Update the Natesha Website

This guide explains how to add or update events and gallery items on the Natesha School website. No coding knowledge is required.

---

## One-Time Setup

### 1. Create a free GitHub account (if you don't have one)

1. Go to **github.com** and click "Sign up"
2. Enter your email address and create a password
3. Follow the steps to verify your email
4. You now have a GitHub account

### 2. Accept the website invitation

The website owner will send you an invitation email from GitHub. Open the email and click **"Accept invitation"**. This gives you permission to make changes to the website.

---

## Logging In to the Editor

1. Open your web browser and go to: **nateshadance.com/keystatic**
2. Click **"Sign in with GitHub"**
3. If this is your first time, GitHub will ask you to authorize the Natesha Website app — click **"Authorize"**
4. You are now in the editor dashboard

You will see two sections: **Events** and **Gallery items**.

---

## Adding an Upcoming Event

1. In the editor, click **"Events"**
2. Click **"Create new entry"** (or the + button)
3. Fill in the fields:
   - **Event name**: The full title (e.g., "Suman Nayak & Students — Spring Recital")
   - **Date**: Click the date picker and choose the event date
   - **Venue / location**: Where it is (e.g., "IFAASD Cultural Hall, San Diego")
   - **More-info link (optional)**: A website link for tickets or more details
   - **Description**: Any extra details about the event
4. Click **"Create"** or **"Save"**

The website rebuilds automatically in about 1–2 minutes. The event will appear on the **Events** page. Once its date has passed, it moves automatically to the **Performance History** section — you don't need to do anything.

---

## Editing or Deleting an Event

1. Click **"Events"** in the editor
2. Click the event you want to change
3. Make your edits and click **"Save"**
4. To delete: open the event and click **"Delete"** (there will be a confirmation step)

---

## Adding a Gallery Item (Photos or Video)

1. Click **"Gallery items"** in the editor
2. Click **"Create new entry"**
3. Fill in the fields:
   - **Title / caption**: A short description (e.g., "Kalakshetra Recital — 2023")
   - **Category**: Choose from the dropdown:
     - *Suman Nayak* — solo performances by the guru
     - *Recitals* — group recitals and annual shows
     - *Productions* — full productions and dance dramas
     - *Solo / Arangetram* — individual student debut performances
     - *Videos* — video recordings
   - **Date**: Approximate date of the performance (optional)
   - **Photos**: Click **"Add Photo"**, then click the upload area to choose an image from your computer. Add as many photos as you like. For each photo, fill in the **Photo description** field (e.g., "Suman Nayak performing Alarippu") — this is important for accessibility.
   - **Video link**: If this is a video gallery item, paste a YouTube or other video URL here
4. Click **"Create"** or **"Save"**

---

## Tips

- **Photo descriptions matter**: Screen readers use them to describe images to visually-impaired visitors. Always fill them in.
- **Your changes go live in 1–2 minutes** after you save. You don't need to press any "publish" or "deploy" button.
- **Past events move themselves**: Events with past dates automatically appear in "Performance History" — you never need to manually archive them.
- **If something looks wrong**: Contact the website developer. Don't worry — you cannot accidentally break anything permanently.

---

## Logging Out

Close your browser tab when you're done. You'll be asked to log in with GitHub again next time.
